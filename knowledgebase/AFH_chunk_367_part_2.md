The airplane should be accelerated to \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{X}}\) (if simulated obstacles are present) or \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{Y}}\) during recovery and climb. Considerable forward elevator/stabilator pressure will be required after the stall recovery as the airplane accelerates to \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{X}}\) or \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{Y}}\) . Appropriate trim input should be anticipated. The flap setting should be reduced from full to approach, or as recommended by the manufacturer. Then, with a positive rate of climb, the landing gear is selected up. The remaining flaps are then retracted as a positive rate- of- climb continues.
