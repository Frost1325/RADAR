MSAs are expressed in feet above mean sea level and normally have a \(25\mathrm{NM}\) radius; however, this radius may be expanded to \(30\mathrm{NM}\) if necessary to encompass the airport landing surfaces. Ideally, a single sector altitude is established and depicted on the plan view of approach charts; however, when necessary to obtain relief from obstructions, the area may be further sectored and as many as four MSAs established. When established, sectors may be no less than \(90^{\circ}\) in spread. MSAs provide 1,000 feet clearance over all obstructions but do not necessarily assure acceptable navigation signal coverage.
