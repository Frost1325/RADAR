## Lift-Off

As \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{X}}\) approaches, the pilot should apply back- elevator pressure until reaching the appropriate \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{X}}\) attitude to ensure a smooth and firm lift- off, or rotation. Since the airplane accelerates more rapidly after lift- off, the pilot should apply additional back- elevator pressure to hold a constant airspeed. After becoming airborne, the pilot will maintain a wings- level climb at \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{X}}\) until all obstacles have been cleared, or if no obstacles are present, until reaching an altitude of at least 50 feet above the takeoff surface. Thereafter, the pilot may lower the pitch attitude slightly and continue the climb at \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{Y}}\) until reaching a safe maneuvering altitude. The pilot should always remember that an attempt to pull the airplane off the ground prematurely, or to climb too steeply, may cause the airplane to settle back to the runway or make contact with obstacles.
