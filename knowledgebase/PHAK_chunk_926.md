## Precession

Precession is the tilting or turning of a gyro in response to a defective force. The reaction to this force does not occur at the point at which it was applied; rather, it occurs at a point that is \(90^{\circ}\) later in the direction of rotation. This principle allows the gyro to determine a rate of turn by sensing the amount of pressure created by a change in direction. The rate at which the gyro precesses is inversely proportional to the speed of the rotor and proportional to the defective force.
