Unlike TERPS, which assesses obstacle clearance beginning at the DER, the OEl net takeoff flight path obstacle assessment begins at the point where the aircraft reaches 35 feet above the runway at the end of the OEl takeoff distance. Therefore, the OEl net takeoff flight path assessment may begin before the DER allowing for the use of a portion of the runway for the OEl climb. The OEl net takeoff flight path obstacle clearance assessment must also account for clearance of the low, close- in obstacles that are noted on the IFR departure procedure, but are not necessarily cleared when complying with the TERPS- based IFR climb gradient.

The OEl net takeoff flight path is unique for each aircraft type and is assessed on each takeoff for the required obstacle clearance directly against those obstacles located beneath the OEl flight track and within the prescribed lateral distance from the flight path centerline. TERPS, on the other hand, provides a required climb gradient that represents a surface that the aircraft's all- engines- operating climb profile must remain above throughout the IFR climb until reaching the en route environment.
