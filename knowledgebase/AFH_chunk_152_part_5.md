The pilot rolls the airplane into a coordinated, level- flight \(45^{\circ}\) turn and then smoothly, firmly, and progressively increase the AOA through back elevator pressure until a stall occurs. Alternatively, the pilot rolls the airplane into a coordinated, level- flight \(45^{\circ}\) turn at an airspeed above \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{A}}\) or \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{O}}\) . After the airspeed slows to \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{A}}\) or \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{O}}\) , and at an airspeed 5 to 10 percent faster than the unaccelerated stall speed, the pilot progressively increases the AOA through back elevator pressure until a stall occurs. The increased back elevator pressure increases lift and the G load. The G load pushes the pilot's body down in the seat.
