A jet airplane can easily exceed its speed limitations. The handling qualities of a jet may change significantly at speeds higher than the maximum allowed.

High- speed airplanes designed for subsonic flight are limited to some Mach number below the speed of sound. Shock waves (and the adverse effects associated with them) can occur when the airplane speed is substantially below Mach 1.0. The Mach number at which some portion of the airflow over the wing first equals Mach 1.0 is termed the critical Mach number ( \(\mathrm{MC_R}\) ).
