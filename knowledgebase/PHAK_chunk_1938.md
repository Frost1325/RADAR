 Illusions

Landing illusions occur in many forms. Above featureless terrain at night, there is a natural tendency to fly a lower- than- normal approach. Elements that cause any type of visual obscurities, such as rain, haze, or a dark runway environment, can also cause low approaches. Bright lights,

steep surrounding terrain, and a wide runway can produce the illusion of being too low with a tendency to fly a higher- than- normal approach. A set of regularly spaced lights along a road or highway can appear to be runway lights. Pilots have even mistaken the lights on moving trains as runway or approach lights. Bright runway or approach lighting systems can create the illusion that the aircraft is closer to the runway, especially where few lights illuminate the surrounding terrain.
