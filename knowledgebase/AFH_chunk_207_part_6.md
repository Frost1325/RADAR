Consistent with previous downwind and crosswind descriptions, the pilot should increase the bank angle as the airplane reaches the downwind position and decrease the bank angle as the airplane reaches the crosswind position. Further, the rate of roll- in and roll- out should be consistent with how fast the grounded changes during the turn. Remember, when turning from an upwind or downwind position to a crosswind position, 1/2 of the grounded change occurs during the first 2/3 of the \(90^{\circ}\) turn. The final 1/2 of the change in grounded occurs during the last 1/3 of the turn. In contrast, when turning from a crosswind position to an upwind or downwind position, the first 1/2 of the grounded change occurs during the first 1/3 of the \(90^{\circ}\) turn. The final 1/2 of the chance in grounded occurs during the last 2/3 of the turn.
