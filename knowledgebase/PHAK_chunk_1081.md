\mathrm{lb}\times 30\mathrm{in} = 3,000\mathrm{in - lb}\)

Moment change \(= [15,000 - 3,000] = 12,000\mathrm{in - lb}\)

By adding the moment change to the original moment (or subtracting if the weight has been moved forward instead of aft), the new total moments are obtained. Then determine the new CG by dividing the new moments by the total weight:

\[{\mathrm{Total~moments}}={\mathrm{Total~moments}}={\mathrm{Total~moments}}={\mathrm{total~moments}}={\mathrm{total~moments}}={\mathrm{total~moments}}.\]

\[\mathrm{CG} = \frac{628,000\mathrm{in - lb}}{8,000\mathrm{lb}} = 78.5\mathrm{in}\]

The shift has caused the CG to shift to station 78.5.
