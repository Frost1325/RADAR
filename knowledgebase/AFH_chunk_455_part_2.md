- \(\mathbf{V}_{\mathrm{EF}}\) -speed used during certification at which the critical engine is assumed to fail.

- \(\mathbf{V}_{\mathrm{R}}\) -rotation speed, or speed at which the rotation of the airplane is initiated to takeoff attitude. This speed cannot be less than \(\mathbf{V}_{\mathrm{I}}\) or less than \(1.05 \times \mathbf{V}_{\mathrm{MCA}}\) (minimum control speed in the air). On a single-engine takeoff, it also allows for the acceleration to \(\mathbf{V}_{\mathrm{2}}\) at the 35-foot height at the end of the runway.

- \(\mathbf{V}_{\mathrm{LOF}}\) -lift-off speed, or speed at which the airplane first becomes airborne. This is an engineering term used when the airplane is certificated to meet certain requirements. The pilot takes this speed into consideration if the AFM lists it.
