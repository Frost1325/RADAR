## Likelihood of an Event

Likelihood of an EventLikelihood is nothing more than taking a situation and determining the probability of its occurrence. It is rated as probable, occasional, remote, or improbable. For example, a pilot is flying from point A to point B (50 miles) in marginal visual flight rules (MVFR) conditions. The likelihood of encountering potential instrument meteorological conditions (IMC) is the first question the pilot needs to answer. The experiences of other pilots, coupled with the forecast, might

cause the pilot to assign "occasional" to determine the probability of encountering IMC.

The following are guidelines for making assignments.

Probable—an event will occur several times Occasional—an event will probably occur sometime Remote—an event is unlikely to occur, but is possible Improbable—an event is highly unlikely to occur
