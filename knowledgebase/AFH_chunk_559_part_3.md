Hydrolaning. A condition that exists when landing on a surface with standing water deeper than the tread depth of the tires. When the brakes are applied, there is a possibility that the brake will lock up and the tire will ride on the surface of the water, much like a water ski. When the tires are hydroplaning, directional control and braking action are virtually impossible. An effective anti- skid system can minimize the effects of hydroplaning.

Hypoxia. A lack of sufficient oxygen reaching the body tissues.
