Before starting any practice maneuver, the pilot ensures that the area is clear of air traffic and other hazards. Further, distant references should be chosen to allow the pilot to assess when to begin rollout from the turn. After establishing the manufacturer's recommended entry speed, \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{A}}\) , or \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{O}}\) , as applicable, the airplane should be smoothly rolled into a predetermined bank angle between \(45^{\circ}\) and \(60^{\circ}\) . As the bank angle is being established, generally prior to \(30^{\circ}\) of bank, elevator back pressure should be smoothly applied to increase the AOA and power should be added. Pilots should keep in mind that as the AOA increases, so does drag, and additional power allows the airplane to maintain airspeed. After the selected bank angle has been reached, the pilot will find that considerable force is required on the elevator control to hold the airplane in level flight.
