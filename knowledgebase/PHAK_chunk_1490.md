Pulsating VASIs normally consist of a single- light unit projecting a two- color visual approach path into the final approach area of the runway upon which the indicator is installed. The "on glidepath" indication is a steady white

light. The "slightly below glidepath" indication is a steady red light. If the aircraft descends further below the glidepath, the red light starts to pulsate. The "above glidepath" indication is a pulsating white light. The pulsating rate increases as the aircraft gets further above or below the desired glideslope. The useful range of the system is about four miles during the day and up to ten miles at night. [Figure 14- 32]
