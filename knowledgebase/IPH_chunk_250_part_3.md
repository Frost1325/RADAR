The visibility published on an approach chart is dependent on many variables, including the height above touchdown for straight- in approaches or height above airport elevation for circling approaches. Other factors include the approach light system coverage, and type of approach procedure,

such as precision, non- precision, circling or straight- in. Another factor determining the minimum visibility is the penetration of the 34:1 and 20:1 surfaces. These surfaces are inclined planes that begin 200 feet out from the runway and extend outward to the DA point (for approaches with vertical guidance), the VDP location (for non- precision approaches) and 10,000 feet for an evaluation to a circling runway. If there is a penetration of the 34:1 surface, the published visibility can be no lower than three- fourths SM. If there is penetration of the 20:1 surface, the published visibility can be no lower than 1 SM with a note prohibiting approaches to the affected runway at night (both straight- in and circling).
