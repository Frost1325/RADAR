The pilot turned to the assigned heading climbing through 5,000 feet. At 5,300 feet, Center advised assigned altitude was 5,000 feet. The pilot immediately descended to 5,000. Center then informed the pilot that there was traffic at 12 o'clock and a mile at 6,000. After passing traffic, a higher altitude was assigned and climb resumed. The pilot then believed the clearance was probably "reaching" 5,000, etc. Even the readback to the controller with "leaving" did not catch the different wording. "Reaching" and "leaving" are commonly used ATC terms having different usages. They may be used in clearances involving climbs, descents, turns, or speed changes. In the flight deck, the words "reaching" and "leaving" sound much alike.
