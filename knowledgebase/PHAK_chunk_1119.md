to altitude (PE); the airspeed decreases to something less than 120 knots as the altitude increases.

There are two primary reasons to evaluate climb performance. First, aircraft must climb over obstacles to avoid hitting them. Second, climbing to higher altitudes can provide better weather, fuel economy, and other benefits. Maximum Angle of Climb (AOC), obtained at \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{X}}\) , may provide climb performance to ensure an aircraft will clear obstacles. Maximum Rate of Climb (ROC), obtained at \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{Y}}\) , provides climb performance to achieve the greatest altitude gain over time. Maximum ROC may not be sufficient to avoid obstacles in some situations, while maximum AOC may be sufficient to avoid the same obstacles. [Figure 11- 7]
