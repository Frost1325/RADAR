## Localizer-Type Directional Aid (LDA)

The LDA is of comparable use and accuracy to a localizer but is not part of a complete ILS. The LDA course usually provides a more precise approach course than the similar simplified directional facility (SDF) installation, which may have a course width of \(6^{\circ}\) or \(12^{\circ}\) .

The LDA is not aligned with the runway. Straight- in minimums may be published where alignment does not exceed \(30^{\circ}\) between the course and runway. Circling minimums only are published where this alignment exceeds \(30^{\circ}\) .

A very limited number of LDA approaches also incorporate a glideslope. These are annotated in the plan view of the instrument approach chart with a note, "LDA/Glideslope." These procedures fall under a newly defined category of approaches called Approach (Procedure) with Vertical Guidance (aviation) APVs. LDA minima for with and without glideslope is provided and annotated on the minima lines of the approach chart as S- LDA/GS and S- LDA.
