The previous description of a lazy eight and figure 10- 4 describe how a lazy eight looks from outside the flight deck and describes it as two \(180^{\circ}\) turns with altitude changes. How does it look from the pilot's perspective? Think of the longitudinal axis of the airplane as a pencil, which draws on whatever it points to. During this maneuver, the longitudinal axis of the airplane traces a symmetrical eight on its side with segments of the eight above and below the horizon, and it takes both \(180^{\circ}\) turns to form both loops of an eight. The first \(90^{\circ}\) of the first \(180^{\circ}\) turn traces the upper portion of one of the loops. The second \(90^{\circ}\) portion of the second \(180^{\circ}\) turn traces the lower portion of that loop at the end of the maneuver.
