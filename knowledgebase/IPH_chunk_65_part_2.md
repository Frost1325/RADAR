The OEl net takeoff flight path represents the actual OEl takeoff flight path that has been degraded by an amount specified by the certification rules to provide

a safety margin for expected variations under operational conditions. Subpart I of Part 121 and Part 135 require that the OEl net takeoff flight path be at least 35 feet above obstacles that are located within the prescribed lateral distance either side of the flight path The actual obstacle clearance capability, under optimum conditions after experiencing an engine failure on takeoff, is equal to the difference between gross and net flight path, plus the additional 35 feet.
