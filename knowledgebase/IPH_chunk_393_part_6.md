Automated Weather Sensor System (AWSS). The AWSS is part of the Aviation Surface Weather Observation Network suite of programs and provides pilots and other users with weather information through the Automated Surface Observing System. The AWSS sensor suite automatically collects, measures, processes, and broadcasts surface weather data.

Automated Weather System. Any of the automated weather sensor platforms that collect weather data at airports and disseminate the weather information via radio and/or landline. The systems currently consist of the Automated Surface Observing System (ASOS), Automated Weather Sensor System (AWSS), and Automated Weather Observation System (AWOS).

Automatic Dependent Surveillance- Broadcast (ADS- B). A surveillance system that continuously broadcasts GPS position information, aircraft identification, altitude, velocity vector, and direction to all other aircraft and air traffic control facilities within a specific area. Automatic dependent surveillance- broadcast (ADS- B) information is displayed in the flight deck via a flight deck display of traffic information (CDTI) unit, providing the pilot with greater situational awareness. ADS- B transmissions also provides controllers with a more complete picture of traffic and updates that information more frequently than other surveillance equipment.
