A standard temperature lapse rate is when the temperature decreases at the rate of approximately \(3.5^{\circ}\mathrm{F}\) or \(2^{\circ}\mathrm{C}\) per thousand feet up to 36,000 feet, which is approximately \(- 65^{\circ}\mathrm{F}\) or \(- 55^{\circ}\mathrm{C}\) . Above this point, the temperature is considered constant up to 80,000 feet. A standard pressure lapse rate is when pressure decreases at a rate of approximately \(1^{\circ}\mathrm{Hg}\) per 1,000 feet of altitude gain to 10,000 feet.
