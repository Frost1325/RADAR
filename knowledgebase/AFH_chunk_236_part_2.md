After attaining the appropriate airspeed and adjusting pitch attitude for a climb, the pilot should "rough trim" the airplane to relieve any adverse control pressures. More precise trim adjustments can be made when flight conditions have stabilized. On airplanes that produce high control pressures when using maximum power on go- arounds, the pilot should use caution when reaching for the flap handle. Airplane control is the first consideration during this high- workload phase.
