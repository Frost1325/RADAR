At least \(5^{\circ}\) and a maximum of \(10^{\circ}\) of bank toward the operative engine should be used initially to stop the yaw and maintain directional control. This initial bank input is held only momentarily, just long enough to establish or ensure directional control. Climb performance suffers when bank angles exceed approximately 2 or \(3^{\circ}\) , but obtaining and maintaining \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{YSE}}\) and directional control are paramount.
