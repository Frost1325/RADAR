If a crosswind exists, the wings should be kept level by displacing the control wheel into the crosswind. During the takeoff roll, the primary responsibility of the pilot not flying is to closely monitor the aircraft systems and to call out the proper V speeds as directed in the captain's briefing.

Slight forward pressure should be held on the control column to keep the nose- wheel rolling firmly on the runway. If nosewheel steering is being utilized, the pilot flying should monitor the nose- wheel steering to about 80 knots (or \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{MCG}}\) for the particular airplane) while the pilot not flying applies the forward pressure. After reaching \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{MCG}}\) , the pilot flying should bring his or her left hand up to the control wheel. The pilot's other hand should be on the thrust levers until at least \(\mathrm{V}_{1}\) speed is attained.
