Speed and altitude restrictions in clearances are subject to misinterpretation, as evidenced in this case where a corporate flight crew treated instructions in a published procedure as a clearance. The aircraft was at FL 310 and had already programmed the 'expect- crossing altitude' of 17,000 feet at the VOR. When the altitude alerter sounded, the pilot advised Center that we were leaving FL 310. ATC acknowledged with a "Roger." At FL 270, Center questioned the pilot about the aircrafts descent. The pilot told the controller that the reason for the descent was to cross the VOR at 17,000 feet. ATC advised the pilot that he did not have clearance to descend.
