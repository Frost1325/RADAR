In general, When the wind is blowing straight into the nose of the airplane, the grounded speed will be less than the airspeed. When the wind is blowing from directly behind the airplane, the grounded speed will be faster than the airspeed. In other words, when the airplane is headed upwind, the grounded speed is decreased; when headed downwind, the grounded speed is increased.
