(\mathbf{V}_{\mathrm{X}}\) -best angle of climb speed—speed at which the airplane gains the greatest altitude for a given distance of forward travel.

- \(\mathbf{V}_{\mathrm{XSE}}\) -best angle-of-climb speed with OEI.

- \(\mathbf{V}_{\mathrm{Y}}\) -best rate of climb speed—speed at which the airplane gains the most altitude for a given unit of time.

- \(\mathbf{V}_{\mathrm{YSE}}\) -best rate of climb speed with OEI. Marked with a blue radial line on most airspeed indicators. Above the single-engine absolute ceiling, \(\mathbf{V}_{\mathrm{YSE}}\) yields the minimum rate of sink.

- \(\mathbf{V}_{\mathrm{SSE}}\) -safe, intentional OEI speed—originally known as safe single-engine speed. It is the minimum speed to intentionally render the critical engine inoperative.
