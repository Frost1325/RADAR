At the edge of the energy envelope, where available \(P_{S} = 0\) at full throttle, the airplane can no longer climb while maintaining airspeed or accelerate without descending. Inside this envelope, inner contours increase in value, reaching a "peak" where available \(P_{S}\) is maximized. Notice that \(P_{S}\) at full throttle is maximized at a specific airspeed ( \(V_{Y}\) ) decreasing in value at slower or faster airspeeds. At \(V_{Y}\) then, the airplane can attain the maximum rate of climb while maintaining airspeed or the maximum acceleration without descending [Figure 4- 7].
