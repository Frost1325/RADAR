## Flight Computers

Up to this point, only mathematical formulas have been used to determine such items as time, distance, speed, and fuel consumption. In reality, most pilots use a mechanical flight computer called an E6B or electronic flight calculator. These devices can compute numerous problems associated with flight planning and navigation. The mechanical or electronic

computer has an instruction book that probably includes sample problems so the pilot can become familiar with its functions and operation. [Figure 16- 18]

## Plotter

Another aid in flight planning is a plotter, which is a protractor and ruler. The pilot can use this when determining TC and measuring distance. Most plotters have a ruler that measures in both NM and SM and has a scale for a sectional chart on one side and a world aeronautical chart on the other. [Figure 16- 18]
