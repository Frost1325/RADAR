Prognostic charts are an excellent source of information for preflight planning; however, this chart should be viewed in light of current conditions and specific local area forecasts.

The 36- and 48- hour significant weather prognostic chart is an extension of the 12- and 24- hour forecast. This chart is issued twice a day. ## ATC Radar Weather Displays

Although ATC systems cannot always detect the presence or absence of clouds, they can often determine the intensity

of a precipitation area, but the specific character of that area (snow, rain, hail, VIRGA, etc.) cannot be determined. For this reason, ATC refers to all weather areas displayed on ATC radar scopes as "precipitation."
