In the absence of a recommended speed, the pilot should use \(1.4 \mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{SO}}\) . When this airspeed is attained, the nose is lowered to maintain the gliding speed and the controls trimmed. The wing flaps may be gradually lowered and the pitch attitude adjusted, as needed, to establish the proper descent angle. The base- to- final turn is planned and accomplished so that upon rolling out of the turn, the airplane is aligned with the runway centerline. If the approach is planned to be slightly high in the current configuration, the pilot will be assured of making the aiming point. The wing flaps may be lowered, as needed, and the pitch attitude adjusted, as needed, to establish the proper descent angle and airspeed ( \(1.3 \mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{SO}}\) ), and the controls trimmed.
