In either case, it is vital to announce intentions and to scan outside. Make course and speed adjustments that will lead to a successful pattern entry and give way to other aircraft on the preferred \(45^{\circ}\) entry or to aircraft already established on downwind.

Why is it advantageous to use the preferred \(45^{\circ}\) entry? If it is not possible to enter the pattern due to conflicting traffic, the pilot on a \(45^{\circ}\) entry can continue to turn away from the downwind, fly a safe distance away, and return for another attempt to join on the \(45^{\circ}\) entry—all while scanning for traffic.
