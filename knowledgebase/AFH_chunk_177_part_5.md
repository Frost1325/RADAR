The pitch attitude that the student has learned to associate with initial climb may also differ due to decreased weight, and the flight controls may seem more sensitive. If the situation is unexpected, it may result in increased anxiety that may remain until after the landing. Frequently, the existence of this anxiety and the uncertainty that develops due to the perception of an "abnormal" takeoff results in poor performance on the subsequent landing.
