 Factors in Steep Turns

At a constant altitude, during a coordinated turn in any aircraft, the load factor is the result of two forces: centrifugal force and weight. [Figure 5- 52] For any given bank angle, the ROT varies with the airspeed—the higher the speed, the

slower the ROT. This compensates for added centrifugal force, allowing the load factor to remain the same.

Figure 5- 53 reveals an important fact about turns—the load factor increases at a terrific rate after a bank has reached \(45^{\circ}\) or \(50^{\circ}\) . The load factor for any aircraft in a coordinated level turn at \(60^{\circ}\) bank is 2 Gs. The load factor in an \(80^{\circ}\) bank is 5.76 Gs. The wing must produce lift equal to these load factors if altitude is to be maintained.
