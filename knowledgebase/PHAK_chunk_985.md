AirspeedAirspeed limitations are shown on the airspeed indicator (ASI) by color coding and on placards or graphs in the aircraft. [Figure 9- 1] A red line on the ASI shows the airspeed limit beyond which structural damage could occur. This is called the never- exceed speed ( \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{NE}}\) ). A yellow arc indicates the speed range between maximum structural cruising speed ( \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{NO}}\) ) and \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{NE}}\) . Operation of an aircraft in the yellow airspeed arc is for smooth air only and then only with caution. A green arc depicts the normal operating speed range, with the upper end at \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{NO}}\) and the lower end at stalling speed at maximum weight with the landing gear and flaps retracted ( \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{S1}}\) ). For airplanes, the flap operating range is depicted by the white arc, with the upper end at the maximum flap extended speed ( \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{FE}}\) ), and the lower end at the stalling speed with the landing gear and flaps in the landing configuration ( \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{S0}}\) ).
