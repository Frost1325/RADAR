\(\mathbf{V}_{\mathrm{A}}\) . The design maneuvering speed. This is the "rough air" speed and the maximum speed for abrupt maneuvers. If during flight, rough air or severe turbulence is encountered, reduce the airspeed to maneuvering speed or less to minimize stress on the airplane structure. It is important to consider weight when referencing this speed. For example, \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{A}}\) may be 100 knots when an airplane is heavily loaded, but only 90 knots when the load is light.

Vapor lock. A condition in which air enters the fuel system and it may be difficult, or impossible, to restart the engine. Vapor lock may occur as a result of running a fuel tank completely dry, allowing air to enter the fuel system. On fuel- injected engines, the fuel may become so hot it vaporizes in the fuel line, not allowing fuel to reach the cylinders.
