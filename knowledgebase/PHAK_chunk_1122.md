## Rate of Climb (ROC)

ROC is a comparison of altitude gained relative to the time needed to reach that altitude. ROC is simply the vertical component of the aircraft's flight path velocity vector. For maximum ROC performance, a pilot flies the aircraft at \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{Y}}\) so as to achieve a maximum gain in altitude over a given period of time.

Maximum ROC expedites a climb to an assigned altitude. This gains the greatest vertical distance over a period of time. For example, in a maximum AOC profile, a certain aircraft takes 30 seconds to reach 1,000 feet AGL, but covers only 3,000 feet over the ground.
