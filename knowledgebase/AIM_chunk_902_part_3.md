2. Flight Management System (FMS) coding of the offset RNAV PRM and GLS PRM approaches in a SOIA operation is different than other RNAV and GLS approach coding in that it does not match the ini

tial procedure published on the charted IAP. In the SOIA design of the offset approach, the lateral course terminates at the fictitious threshold point (FTP), which is an extension of the final approach course to a point near the runway threshold. The FTP is designated in the approach coding as the MAP so that vertical guidance is available to the pilot to the runway threshold, just as vertical guidance is provided by the LDA glideslope. RNAV and GLS lateral guidance, in contrast, is discontinued at the charted MAP and replaced by visual maneuvering to accomplish runway alignment in the same manner as LDA course guidance is discontinued at the MAP.
