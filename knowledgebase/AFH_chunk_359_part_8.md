The airplane should be allowed to accelerate in a shallow climb to attain \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{Y}}\) , the best all- engine rate- of- climb speed. \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{Y}}\) should then be maintained until achieving a safe single- engine maneuvering altitude, which considers terrain and obstructions. Any speed above or below \(\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{Y}}\) reduces the performance of the airplane. Even with all engines operating normally, terrain and obstruction clearance during the initial climb after takeoff is an important preflight consideration. Most airliners and most turbine- powered airplanes climb out at an attitude that yields best rate of climb \((\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{Y}})\) usually utilizing a flight management system (FMS).

When to raise the landing gear after takeoff depends on several factors. Normally, the gear should be retracted when there is insufficient runway available for landing and after a positive rate of climb is established as indicated on the altimeter.
