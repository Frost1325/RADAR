Additional forecasts may be inserted during the first 6 hours (for example, 01, 02, 04, and 05). 00 hour represents the initial conditions, and the subsequent graphics depict the area affected by the particular hazard at that valid time. Forecasts valid at 00 through 06 hours correspond to the text AIRMET bulletin. Forecasts valid at 06 through 12 hours correspond to the text bulletin outlook. G-AIRMET depicts the following en route aviation weather hazards:

(a) Instrument flight rule conditions (ceiling \(< 1000^{\circ}\) and/or surface visibility \(< 3\) miles)
(b) Mountain obscuration
(c) Icing
(d) Freezing level

(e) Turbulence
(f) Low level wind shear (LLWS)
(g) Strong surface winds
